Planning a trip or upgrading your home? Here is a simple guide to the most widely used electrical outlets worldwide, their voltages, and safety features.

Deep Dive: The 6 Socket Standards

1. Type A & B (North American Standards)

Type A features two flat parallel pins, while Type B adds a round grounding pin for safety. These operate primarily at 110–120 Volts.

  • Safety Tip: Type A plugs fit into Type B sockets, but not vice-versa.

2. Type C (Europlug)

Known as the universal 2-pin plug across Europe and parts of Asia. It is ungrounded and limited to low-amp devices like phone chargers.

3. Type G (British Standard BS 1363)

Widely considered one of the safest socket designs globally. It includes an internal fuse, shuttered safety slots, and a sturdy 3-prong layout.
