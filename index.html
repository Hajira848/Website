Socket types

An overview of 6 standard mains wall outlets used worldwide for mobile charging and home appliances.

Power Delivery: Different countries use different physical configurations for wall outlets to safely deliver alternating current (AC) electricity to consumer electronics.
Type A

North American Flat-Pin

An ungrounded utility outlet standard with two thin slots. It is primarily used for small, double-insulated electronic devices and mobile chargers across North America and Japan.

Pins:Two flat parallel pins
Common in:United States, Canada, Japan
Voltage:Usually used for 100–127 V supplies
Type B

Grounded American

A grounded utility standard that builds on the Type A structure by adding a round grounding pin. It provides extra safety protection for high-power appliances and heavy computer devices.

Pins:Two flat parallel pins with one round grounding pin
Common in:United States, Canada, Mexico
Design:Grounded version of Type A
Type C

Europlug Socket

The universal, ungrounded round-pin design known as the Europlug. It features a flat, sleek profile and is widely used across Europe and parts of Asia for low-power consumer gadgets.

Pins:Two round pins
Common in:Most European countries, parts of Asia and Africa
Design:Used for low-power devices
Type F

Schuko Socket

A round, recessed plug system featuring dual side-earthing clips instead of a pin. Known as the 'Schuko' standard, it is robustly built for medium to large European home appliances.

Pins:Two round pins with two earth clips on the side
Common in:Germany, Austria, Netherlands, Sweden, Norway
Design:Grounded version of Type C, rated for 16 A
Type G

British Standard

A heavy-duty rectangular 3-pin configuration featuring internal safety shutters. It is highly secure, requires a grounding element to unlock the outlet, and contains an internal fuse.

Pins:Three rectangular pins
Common in:United Kingdom, Ireland, UAE, Singapore, Malaysia
Design:Includes a built-in fuse for added safety
Type J

Swiss Standard

A slim, hexagon-profile wall interface unique to Switzerland. It accommodates standard Type C plugs smoothly while offering structural grounding through an offset third pin.

Pins:Three round pins (2 parallel + 1 offset ground)
Common in:Switzerland, Liechtenstein
Voltage:Designed for 230 V supplies
